CONSTITUTION OF THE KINGDOM OF THE LEVANT

PREAMBLE

We, the people of the Levantine Kingdom, united in our diversity and committed to the principles of equality, justice, and peace, hereby establish this Constitution to secure the rights and freedoms of all individuals within our nation.

SECTION 1: CITIZENSHIP

1.1 Any person may be granted citizenship or allowance for entry into the Levantine Kingdom without discrimination. Discrimination includes, but is not limited to:

1.1.1 Providing preference to members or non-members of any group outlined in Section 4.1.

1.1.2 Disallowing entry to the nation for members of any aforementioned group.

1.1.3 Showing contempt or discrimination towards individuals in matters of travel and immigration, both within and outside our country.

1.2 All citizens and visitors are entitled to a set of rights outlined in Section 4.

SECTION 2: LANGUAGE

2.1 The Levantine Kingdom respects the freedom of language. Discrimination based on the language spoken by individuals is strictly prohibited under Section 4.1.

2.2 Some languages may be designated as "official" within an Ensate (see Section 3), leading to their use for enacting laws. When an official language is established within an Ensate, it becomes official for the entire nation.

2.3 Official languages at the national level are promoted but not required for translations of this Constitution, subsequent additions to it, or nationwide laws.

SECTION 3: ORGANIZATION AND GOVERNMENT

3.1 The Levantine Kingdom is organized into Ensates, each ruled by an Ensi. All Ensis must pledge loyalty to the King of the Levant and abide by laws enacted by the national parliament or the King.

3.2 The selection process for an Ensi may vary within each Ensate.

3.3 An Ensi is a legal entity and must represent the interests of the entire Ensate. Upon assuming the position, every Ensi automatically becomes a citizen of the Levantine Kingdom.

3.4 The Parliament consists of all Ensis and a democratically elected group of Parliamentarians. Each Ensate is entitled to a minimum of one Parliamentarian, with an additional Parliamentarian for every three individuals beyond that.

SECTION 4: RIGHTS OF ALL

4.1 No person shall be subjected to legal discrimination or punishment based on the following, as well as any other grounds not listed herein:

4.1.1 Race

4.1.2 Religion or Culture

4.1.3 Language

4.1.4 Sexuality

4.1.5 Gender

4.1.6 Any others not listed.

4.2 Every individual has the right to a fair trial for any offense listed in this Constitution or any other laws. However, the Levantine Kingdom reserves the right to detain individuals until their trial.

4.3 Regardless of conviction, no person, including opposing military forces and criminals, shall be subject to unjust treatment, including but not limited to the following:

4.3.1 Holding in tighter custody than necessary under the law to prevent similar incidents, such as:

4.3.1.1 Torture through continuous teleportation or confinement in a dangerous area.

4.3.1.2 Holding hostages for purposes of bribery beyond what is permitted by law.

4.3.2 Deprivation of essential sustenance required for survival.

4.4 Every citizen has the right to vote for their Parliamentarian.

4.5 Every person, regardless of citizenship, has the right to freedom of speech that does not cause undue harm to others.

4.6 Every person has the right to be free from unnecessary harm, including punishment beyond what is permitted by law or assault by another individual.

4.7 Any violation of these rights against another person shall be considered a crime, in addition to any offenses listed as crimes in the laws of the Levantine Kingdom.

IN CONCLUSION

We, the people of the Levantine Kingdom, adopt and uphold this Constitution as the foundation of our nation. With the spirit of unity and respect, we strive to safeguard the rights, freedoms, and dignity of all individuals within our borders, promoting a just and harmonious society for present and future generations.
